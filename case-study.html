Customer-led Product Adaptation

8 Gauge Short — 4.00 × 350 mm

A practical electrode format developed around actual consumption patterns in cylinder fabrication and similar piping work.

The working challenge

In hilly markets and parts of eastern Uttar Pradesh, users involved in cylinder fabrication reported inefficient consumption of the conventional 4.00 × 450 mm electrode. The working cycle often left an avoidable unused portion, increasing material wastage and operating cost.

The issue was not simply the electrode classification. It was the relationship between electrode length, actual weld run and the way the product was being consumed in that fabrication environment.

Product value begins with the work being performed.

A small dimensional change can improve handling and material utilisation when it reflects the user’s actual process.

01 · Observe

Consumption pattern

The standard 4.00 × 450 mm format was reviewed against the weld lengths and handling pattern common in the target application.

02 · Adapt

Optimised length

Kamla Electrodes introduced a 4.00 × 350 mm electrode intended to align more closely with actual consumption in cylinder and similar piping fabrication.

03 · Deliver

Market-ready format

Packaging and distribution were organised around the shorter format, marketed locally as “aath number chota”.

Product Response

Shorter format, more purposeful utilisation

The 4.00 × 350 mm configuration was developed to reduce the unused electrode length associated with the original working pattern. The shorter format also offered a familiar 4.00 mm core diameter while changing the quantity of consumable presented in each rod.

  • Reduced avoidable electrode wastage in the identified application
  • Better alignment between rod length and common weld runs
  • Potential improvement in material utilisation and operating cost
  • A distinct, easily communicated market format
  • Packaging and distribution planned around the specialised size
