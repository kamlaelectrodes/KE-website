Storage & Handling Basics

  • Store electrodes in a dry, moisture-controlled area.
  • Protect packaging integrity to avoid contamination.
  • Follow first-in-first-out stock discipline.

Covered electrodes can be affected by careless storage, moisture exposure, broken packaging and rough handling. Keep cartons away from direct floor contact, rain splash, chemical contamination and open dust. For trade counters and workshops, FIFO discipline helps reduce old stock accumulation and keeps customer experience consistent.

Where a specific product requires baking or rebaking, follow product-specific guidance rather than generic assumptions.

Selection Guidance

  • Match size to joint design, base material and job requirement.
  • Use low hydrogen range for critical conditions where applicable.
  • Consult for hardfacing, repair and alloy jobs.

Electrode choice depends on the job, not just the brand name. Key factors include base metal, thickness, welding position, machine type, polarity, joint preparation, expected loading and finish requirement. For general mild steel fabrication, E6013 type electrodes are common. For critical structures, repair work, higher strength steels or low-hydrogen requirements, technical confirmation is preferred.

Current Setting & Arc Behaviour

Use current range as a starting point, then refine based on actual weld response.

Too low a current may create unstable arc, poor fusion and excessive slag interference. Too high a current may cause spatter, undercut, burn-through or overheated coating. Operators should check arc stability, bead appearance, penetration, slag detachability and heat input before continuing long production welds.

For Kmatic X-45 packaging reference, 3.15 × 350 mm is shown around 100–130 A and 4.00 × 450 mm around 140–180 A, subject to machine and job conditions.

Troubleshooting Support

Share photos, base material, electrode size and weld parameters for faster help.

For porosity, arc instability, slag inclusion, bead appearance issues, cracking or unusual spatter, useful details include electrode brand, size, batch if visible, current range, polarity, welding position, base material, joint gap, surface condition and storage history. A clear photo of the bead, joint and packet label usually reduces back-and-forth.

Safety Essentials

Welding safety must be treated as part of the product usage process.

  • Keep your head out of fumes.
  • Use enough ventilation or exhaust near the arc to keep fumes and gases away from breathing zone.
  • Wear correct eye, ear and body protection.
  • Do not touch live electrical parts.
  • Read and understand product labels before use.

What to share for faster technical support

A complete application snapshot helps the team provide practical guidance faster.

  • Size, current setting and welding position.
  • Base material, joint type and application.
  • Photos of weld bead, packaging or issue area.
  • Storage condition and approximate age of opened packet if relevant.
