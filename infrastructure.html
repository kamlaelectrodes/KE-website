Infrastructure

Manufacturing infrastructure built around controlled preparation, layered inspection and dependable dispatch readiness—not merely end-stage checking.

Built for process discipline

Kamla Electrodes operates from its manufacturing base in Achronda Industrial Area, Partapur, Meerut. The plant supports the essential stages of covered electrode production: incoming material verification, wire drawing and sizing, straightening and cutting, coating preparation, extrusion, drying and baking, inspection, packing and dispatch coordination.

The public website explains the process architecture without publishing proprietary formulations, equipment settings or internal acceptance limits. What buyers can see is the logic behind the product: quality is built stage by stage, with internal checks intended to catch variation before it reaches the next operation.

What happens at each stage

01

Incoming material verification

The process begins before production starts. Core wire and coating constituents are received, identified and checked against the intended product route. Material identity, physical condition and release status matter because unsuitable input can create variation that no final inspection can completely undo.

Internal control logic: material is treated as controlled process input, not anonymous stock. Release to production follows defined checks and traceability discipline.
02

Wire drawing and sizing

Drawing means pulling wire through progressively smaller dies to reduce and control its diameter. It is done to obtain the required core-wire size, improve dimensional consistency and prepare the wire for stable downstream handling. Diameter variation can affect coating balance, current-carrying behaviour and finished electrode geometry.

Why it matters: controlled wire size creates the consistent foundation required for coating concentricity, electrical behaviour and predictable packing.
03

Straightening and cutting

Drawn wire is straightened and cut into defined electrode lengths. Straightness supports smooth feeding through coating equipment, while length control supports product uniformity, correct pack configuration and expected operator handling.

Internal control logic: dimensional checks are applied before coating so a preventable geometry issue does not travel deeper into production.
04

Flux preparation and batch control

The coating system is prepared from selected constituents according to the intended electrode type. Mixing quality influences extrusion behaviour, coating strength, arc characteristics, slag formation and finished surface condition. Uniform blending is therefore a controlled manufacturing stage, not simply material preparation.

Confidentiality boundary: formulations and operating parameters remain proprietary; the website explains the control philosophy without disclosing the recipe.
05

Extrusion, coating and end finishing

During extrusion, the prepared coating is applied around the core wire. The objective is a continuous and sufficiently uniform coating envelope with controlled concentricity and surface condition. End finishing prepares the grip and striking end according to the product format.

Why it matters: coating uniformity contributes to balanced burning, stable arc behaviour and a repeatable operator experience.
06

Drying and baking

Freshly coated electrodes require controlled drying and, where applicable, baking to stabilise the coating system and manage moisture. Time, temperature and handling discipline are matched to the product route. This stage helps the coating achieve suitable strength and storage readiness before packing.

Internal control logic: moisture control is managed as part of manufacturing quality and then protected through packaging and storage guidance.
07

Inspection, packing and dispatch readiness

Finished electrodes move through dimensional, appearance, coating and packing checks before batch release. Packaging protects the consumable during storage and movement, while batch identification and commercial coordination support traceability and dependable fulfilment.

Release philosophy: final inspection confirms the work of earlier controls; it does not replace them.

Electrode structure overview

In shielded metal arc welding, the electrode core contributes filler metal while the flux coating supports shielding and slag formation during arc action. This is why core-wire consistency, coating integrity, concentricity, drying, storage and handling all matter to finished product reliability.
