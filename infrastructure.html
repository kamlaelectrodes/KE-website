Operational Infrastructure

Infrastructure

A connected manufacturing system for core-wire preparation, coating, drying, inspection, packing, storage and dispatch readiness.

Manufacturing base in Partapur, Meerut

Kamla Electrodes operates from Achronda Industrial Area, Partapur. The plant supports the principal stages of covered-electrode production and the commercial coordination required to move finished products into distribution and industrial supply channels.

The production layout links material handling, wire preparation, coating operations, controlled drying, inspection, packet preparation, finished-goods storage and dispatch so responsibility moves with the material from one stage to the next.

Covered-electrode production flow

The manufacturing route is organised as a sequence of controlled operations. Product-specific settings vary, but the operational structure remains consistent.

1Material Receipt & IdentificationCore wire and coating inputs are received, identified and assigned to the intended product route.
2Wire Drawing & SizingWire is reduced and controlled to the required core diameter.
3Straightening & CuttingPrepared wire is aligned and cut to specified electrode lengths.
4Coating PreparationCoating constituents are blended for the intended electrode class and extrusion route.
5Extrusion & End FinishingCoating is applied around the core wire and electrode ends are prepared.
6Drying & BakingMoisture is managed and the coating is stabilised before packing.
7Inspection & ReleaseFinished electrodes are reviewed, identified and released to packing.
8Packing & DispatchPackets are protected, labelled, stored and coordinated for supply.
Product Outcome

Infrastructure is valuable when it strengthens the electrode.

The plant is not presented as a collection of machines for its own sake. Its purpose is to support the product qualities buyers depend on: dimensional consistency, sound coating, controlled moisture, reliable packing and repeatable behaviour in use.

That product experience is the practical evidence of manufacturing capability. Buyers should receive a dependable consumable, useful commercial support and value that stands up in regular work.

Coating operations

Inspection support

Dimensional, appearance, coating, packing and applicable weld-performance checks support batch release.

Packing & storage

Packet preparation, labels, moisture protection and finished-goods handling support product condition through supply.

Dispatch coordination

Order mix, quantity, documentation, loading and buyer-managed or quoted logistics are aligned before movement.

Operational controls around the plant

01

Material identification and storage

Inputs are received, identified and arranged so the correct material reaches the correct production route.

02

Dimensional preparation

Core-wire diameter, straightness and length influence coating balance, operator handling and pack uniformity.

03

Coating and surface condition

Coating continuity, concentricity, finish and electrode-end preparation are monitored through the coating route.

04

Moisture management

Drying, baking, cooling, storage and packing are coordinated to protect coating condition.

05

Batch identity and packet information

Product name, size, batch details and packing information connect the physical product to production and commercial records.

06

Finished-goods and dispatch planning

Finished-goods coordination supports correct picking, loading, documentation and route planning.
