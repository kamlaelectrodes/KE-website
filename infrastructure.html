Covered electrode production flow

The sequence below shows how the product moves from incoming material to market-ready electrode. Each stage feeds the next; a variation left uncontrolled upstream can influence coating quality, burning behaviour, packing condition or user experience downstream.

1Incoming Material VerificationCore wire and coating inputs checked before release.
2Wire Drawing & SizingWire reduced and controlled to required diameter.
3Straightening & CuttingWire aligned and cut to specified electrode length.
4Flux PreparationCoating constituents blended for process consistency.
5Extrusion & FinishingCoating applied concentrically around the core wire.
6Drying & BakingMoisture controlled and coating system stabilised.
7Inspection, Packing & DispatchBatch reviewed, protected and released for supply.

What happens at each stage

01

Incoming material verification

The process begins before production starts. Core wire and coating constituents must be received, identified and checked against the intended product route. Material identity, physical condition and release status matter because unsuitable input can create variation that no final inspection can completely undo.

Internal control logic: material is treated as process input, not anonymous stock. Release to production follows defined checks and traceability discipline.
02

Wire drawing and sizing

Drawing means pulling wire through progressively smaller dies to reduce and control its diameter. It is done to obtain the required core-wire size, improve dimensional consistency and prepare the wire for stable downstream handling. Diameter variation can affect coating balance, current-carrying behaviour and finished electrode geometry.

Why it matters: controlled wire size creates a consistent foundation for coating concentricity and predictable packing.
03

Straightening and cutting

Drawn wire is straightened and cut into defined electrode lengths. Straightness supports smooth feeding through coating equipment, while length control supports product uniformity, correct pack configuration and expected operator handling.

Internal control logic: dimensional checks are applied before coating so a preventable geometry issue does not travel deeper into the process.
04

Flux preparation and batch control

The coating system is prepared from selected constituents according to the intended electrode type. Mixing quality influences extrusion behaviour, coating strength, arc characteristics, slag formation and finished surface condition. Uniform blending is therefore a process-control step, not simply material preparation.

Confidentiality boundary: formulation details and operating parameters remain proprietary; the website explains the control philosophy without disclosing the recipe.
05

Extrusion, coating and end finishing

During extrusion, the prepared coating is applied around the core wire. The objective is a continuous, sufficiently uniform coating envelope with controlled concentricity and surface condition. End finishing prepares the grip and striking end according to the product format.

Why it matters: coating uniformity contributes to balanced burning, usable arc behaviour and reliable operator experience.
06

Drying and baking

Freshly coated electrodes require controlled drying and, where applicable, baking to stabilise the coating system and manage moisture. Time, temperature and handling discipline must suit the product route. This stage helps the coating achieve suitable strength and storage readiness before packing.

Internal control logic: moisture control is managed as part of manufacturing quality, then protected through packaging and storage guidance.
07

Inspection, packing and dispatch readiness

Finished electrodes move through dimensional, appearance, coating and packing checks before batch release. Packaging protects the consumable during storage and movement, while batch identification and commercial coordination support traceability and dependable fulfilment.

Release philosophy: final inspection confirms the work of earlier controls; it is not expected to replace them.
Process Capability Mindset

Beyond pass-or-fail compliance

Six Sigma is a statistical framework for reducing process variation and defects. A formal sigma-level claim requires defined critical-to-quality characteristics, measured opportunities, a validated sample period and auditable calculations. We do not publish an unsupported numerical sigma score.

What Kamla Electrodes can state confidently is that internal process control is prevention-led and layered. BIS requirements are treated as the compliance framework, while internal checks are designed to control the process before final inspection. In plain language: compliance is the floor; consistency is the operating objective.

Electrode structure overview

In shielded metal arc welding, the electrode core contributes filler metal while the flux coating supports shielding and slag formation during arc action. This is why core-wire consistency, coating integrity, concentricity, drying, storage and handling all matter to finished product reliability.
