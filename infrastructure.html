Typical covered electrode production flow

A covered welding electrode is not merely a wire rod with coating. It is a controlled consumable where core wire preparation, coating uniformity, drying, packing and storage all influence usability and consistency.

1. Wire PreparationCore wire selection, straightening and cutting discipline.
2. Coating MixFlux ingredients prepared for coating consistency.
3. ExtrusionCoating applied over the core wire.
4. Drying / BakingMoisture control and coating stabilisation.
5. InspectionDimensional, appearance and packing checks.
6. DispatchBatch-ready supply for buyers and distributors.

Electrode structure overview

In shielded metal arc welding, the electrode core contributes filler metal while the flux coating helps protect the molten weld pool by generating shielding gases and slag during arc action. This is why coating integrity, concentricity, storage and handling are important to product reliability.
